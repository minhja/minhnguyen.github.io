Our latest paper titled **The role of hydration in the removal of glyphosate (GLY) and aminomethylphosphonic acid (AMPA) by nanofiltration membranes** has been accepted for publication in Nature Communications journal (2025 IF of 15.7). 

This paper constitutes the fourth and final Ph.D. chapter of Dr. Phuong Bich Trinh, who began at IAMT-KIT as a Master intern supervised by me. The paper discusses the hydration of pesticide molecules, glyphosate and its metabolite AMPA, and how it affects the removal of these micropollutants by nanofiltration.

To uncover reveal the removal mechanism associated with hydration (dielectric exclusion), we investigate a range of nanofiltration membranes with varying surface chemistry and molecular weight cut-off under different operating conditions.

Dielectric exclusion was strongly suggested – although it was impossible to separate fully from charge (Donnan) exclusion – at low solution pH, at which the micropollutants have close to neutral charge.

The strength of the hydration layer was thoroughly characterized by external collaborators who are experts in infra-red spectroscopy and molecular dynamics simulations.

I contributed to experimental design and many discussions in the review phase on the topic I am passionate about: micropollutant transport in nanofiltration membranes.
