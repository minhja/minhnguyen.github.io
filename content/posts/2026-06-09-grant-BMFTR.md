This is an exciting news. I have officially achieved my first (big) competitive grant titled **Enhancing Boron and Micropollutant Removal from Water by Molecular Layer Deposition Membranes (EMPOWERED)**. This is a BMFTR-MOST collaborative project between Germany (Prof. Andrea I. Schäfer and I, KIT) and Israel (Prof. Tamar Segal-Peretz, Technion).

I received the announcement that the project was funded from the Israeli side in December 2025. However, the German side still required a lot of technical revisions and administrative clearances. Today, BMFTR issued the formal funding notice (Zuwendungsbescheid). After around six months, the adrenaline has died out, but I am happy, so far so good.

The project will start on 1st July 2026. I will serve as project coordinator and my salary will be covered by this grant for two and a half years. This grant also subsidises a PhD student from Technion to research at IAMT-KIT for six months.

Note that the grant information will be published online, but I expect another wait of at least six months!

Abbreviations: *BMFTR* – Federal Ministry of Research, Technology and Space of Germany; *MOST* – Ministry of Innovation, Science and Technology of Israel.
