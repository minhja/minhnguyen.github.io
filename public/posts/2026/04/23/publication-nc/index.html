New publication in Nature Communications

23 April 2026

Our latest paper titled The role of hydration in the removal of glyphosate (GLY) and aminomethylphosphonic acid (AMPA) by nanofiltration membranes has been accepted for publication in Nature Communications journal (2025 IF of 15.7).

This paper marks the fourth and final Ph.D. chapter of Dr. Phuong Bich Trinh, an IAMT-KIT colleague who also comes from Vietnam. Phuong carried out all the experiments and painstalking sample analysis with the LC/MS/MS. I contributed to the experimental design and sharpened up discussions.

What the paper is about

We discuss how hydration governs the nanofiltration of small and charged pesticide micropollutants – glyphosate, the most widely used pesticide, and its metabolite AMPA.

To isolate the removal associated with the shredding of the water shells (dielectric exclusion), six nanofiltration membranes with varying surface chemistry and molecular weight cut-off are experimented under different operating conditions.

At low pH, where the pesticides have close to neutral charge and charge (Donnan) exclusion is minimized, we observe clear evidence that the strongly bound water molecules hinder the pesticide transport.

The strength of the water shells is further characterized by collaborators who are experts in infra-red spectroscopy and molecular dynamics (MD) simulations.

Indeed, our results underscore the importance of hydration in micropollutant nanofiltration and offer insights for enhancing water purification membranes.
